My Dad is not selfish. He is giving, always doing nice things for me and my family, and always puts everyone first before
himself. He is patient, no matter the situation he is always dependable, calm, and understanding.
My dad grew up in a family of 4 kids. He is the oldest son with two older sisters and a younger brother. My dad is a shorter
guy with a kind face and a warm presence. He is often a little unshaven, giving him a relaxed look that matches his easygoing
personality. During the week, he's almost always in khakis and looking neat, but never overly formal. On the weekends he is usually
wearing a shirt representing one of my or my sibling's sports teams, whether it's MHS, Stallions, or MBSA, proudly showing his
support wherever he goes. I feel happy when my dad supports me and my sibling's sports. He is always my cheerleader for
watching me cheer at football games.
When I was in 1st grade, cheering at football games, my Dad would always come to the football game to watch me cheer.
My Dad would always wave, and smile at me. His eyes followed me through the routine, his smile never left his face. He always told
me "I'm proud of you, and I could hear you cheer." I remember that feeling, knowing my dad was there, watching me and believing
in me. Seeing him in the crowd gave me a sense of comfort and confidence. Just knowing he's there makes me feel supported and
proud.
Now many years later, as a freshman in High school, my dad would still come to the football game and watch me cheer.
The games are bigger now and stands are full. My dad would say, "Work Hard, but don't forget to enjoy life." My dad has proud
smiles, and he would always take photos and video our routine. I can tell he is focusing on the field watching the football move
down the field, and keeping track of the game but also watching me cheering. Seeing him there still gives me the same feeling as
1st grade that he is watching me and being proud of me.
At age 15, now a Sophomore in High School, sometimes when I'm watching a football game, I don't always understand
what is happening. The plays, the calls, and all the rules can get a little confusing, but what makes it special is being with my dad.
He just starts explaining what's going on, telling me what team is on defense and offense, and also breaking it down in a way that
makes sense to me. We always get to watch football together on Sundays. Sometimes I ask him "Can we watch the football games
today?" My dad would get happy to see me want to watch with him. My dad gets into the game with the passion of a true fan, and
seeing how much he loves the game makes me enjoy it too. It's not really about football for me, it is about spending time with my
dad and sharing something that he enjoys.
After seeing my dad watch me cheer on at football games, I also got to learn more about football. Sometimes I think about
how one day, I'll be the one in the stands, maybe cheering for my own kids. I'll look back at these moments with my dad feeling
supported and knowing he was proud.
